StompNotes1

We’ve all been in this situation:

You’re reading a paragraph of text, minding your own business, when along comes a little super-imposed number2 directing you to stop what you’re doing and reference a note somewhere else. If you stop to look up the note, however, your focus will suffer. You can try to ignore it and tell yourself that you'll check or just keep on reading so as not to interrupt your flow, but in the back of your mind, you can’t help but wonder what kind of occult knowledge that little footnote professes to hold the answers to.

Footnotes shouldn’t require that kind of mental effort.

If they did, they wouldn’t be footnotes, they’d just be notes. Am I right, or am I right3? It’s either worth knowing, or it isn’t. The problem, though, is that you can’t really know whether or not something is worth knowing until after you already know it4, so why make the reader work any harder than necessary to learn it in the first place?

Information wants to be free!!

So stop obfuscating it. StompNotes is meant to provide a means to make footnotes more easily accessible by placing them alongside their referrer, rather than in an entirely different section of the page, all without sacrificing your beautiful markup5. For pages that are too narrow to accommodate the footnotes on the side, StompNotes responds accordingly(actually, in such cases, it doesn't really do much of anything). StompNotes also degrades gracefully for search engines, screen readers and other scenarios where CSS and JavaScript capabilities are not appropriate.

How it works.

Pretty well6, thanks for asking.

All it does is apply a class of stomped to your body tag and absolutely positions your footnotes so that they line up with their referrers(unless two notes would overlap, in which case they will follow one after another)

Most of the fun stuff is nothing more than some basic CSS applied to your footnotes when the stomped class is present and the browser matches a set of media query conditions to make sure the footnotes can actually fit.

On the agenda

We’re still in the early stages here. StompNotes is just barely past a proof-of-concept at this point.

  • Probably support for a simpler footnote format than the one used in Markdown
  • Support for loading footnotes from a separate page, for longer documents that keep an index of footnotes in their own section
  • Distribute by some means other than this.
  • Keep footnotes from back-to-back referrers from overlapping one another
  • Maybe more7
